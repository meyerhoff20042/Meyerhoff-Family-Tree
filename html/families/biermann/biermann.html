~ The Biermann Family ~

The Biermann family first arrived to the United States in the mid-1840s. Once settling in Randolph County, Illinois, the Biermanns quickly spread across the region as the family grew larger and larger. The first Biermanns in the U.S. were Heinrich Friedrich Biermann (1818-1915) and Sophie Dorothee Fischer (1812-1882) and their daughter Wilhelmina (1843-1925).

Heinrich Friedrich Biermann was the fifth child and second son of Catharina Margarethe Hoermann (1778-1852) and Jürgen Heinrich Biermann (1777-1856), born December 20, 1818, in the town of Wenden in Hanover. I believe his other siblings were Catherine Dorothee Louise Biermann (1804-1873), Marie Catharina Dorothee (Biermann) Hogrefe (1806-1873), Johann Heinrich Biermann (1811-1893), Sophie Dorothee Louise (Biermann) Runge (1814-1872), Catherine Dorothee Biermann (Mar. 1821 - Apr. 1821), and Heinrich Christian Friedrich Biermann (1825-1896). Similar to the Meyerhoff family, all German records of the Biermanns come from church baptism, marriage, and death records. Heinrich married Sophie Fischer on March 26, 1843, in the town of Husum in Hanover. Once moving to Illinois he began farming in the Randolph County area. He passed away on September 13, 1915, in Welge, IL, at the age of 96, from pneumonia and cardiac failure.

Sophie Dorothee Charlotte Fischer was the daughter of Anna Katharina Hoffmaier (1776-????) and Johann Wilhelm Fischer (1777-1843), born November 16, 1814, in the town of Döhren in Hanover. She moved to the U.S. with her husband and daughter and raised their family on the Biermann farm. The couple celebrated 39 years of marriage when Sophie passed away on March 4, 1882, in St. Louis, MO. The couple were buried in Saint John's Evangelical Lutheran Cemetery in Bremen, IL.

Together Heinrich and Sophie had the seven children listed below:

  1. Wilhelmina (Biermann) Oppermann (1843-1925)
    2. Wilhelmina was the first child of Heinrich and Sophie, born April 29, 1843, in Hanover. She moved with her parents to the U.S. in the mid-1840s and married Johann Heinrich August Oppermann (1831-1909) on August 15, 1862, in Bremen, IL. Together they had 12 children:
    1. Ferdinand Adolph Oppermann (Jun. 1863 - Jul. 1863)
    2. Heinrich Wilhelm Oppermann (1864-1948)
    3. Christine Sophie Alvina (Oppermann) Lindner (1866-1926)
    4. Marie Caroline "Lena" (Oppermann) Meyer (1868-1937)
    5. Christine Dorothea Oppermann (1870-1876)
    6. Elise Oppermann (1872-1874)
    7. Herman Heinrich F. Oppermann (1874-1913)
    8. Sophie Dorothea "Dora" (Oppermann) Fricke (1876-1950)
    9. Charles Herman Oppermann (1878-1933)
    10. George Henry Oppermann (1880-1962)
    11. Herman Henry Oppermann (1883-1952)
    12. Mary Caroline (Oppermann) Brockmeyer (1885-1965).
    The couple celebrated 47 years of marriage when Johann passed away on October 30, 1909, in Randolph County, at the age of 77. Wilhelmina passed away 16 years later on December 11, 1925, in Wine Hill, at the age of 82. They were buried in Saint Paul Lutheran Cemetery in Wine Hill, IL.

  2. Maria Louise Ernestina Biermann (1844-1845)
    3. Maria was the second child and second daughter of Heinrich and Sophie, born December 25, 1844, in Germany. She passed away on June 14, 1845, in Germany, at only five months old. As her short life was entirely in Germany, it's hard to find information on her. She is likely buried in a local cemetery near Wenden or Linsburg.

  3. Friedrich Heinrich "Henry" Biermann (1846-1938)
    4. Friedrich was the third child and second son of Heinrich and Sophie, born April 19, 1846, in Welge, IL. He was the first natural-born U.S. citizen of the Biermann family. He married Caroline Christie Sophie Knop (1853-1943) on November 18, 1870, in Bremen. Together they had five children:
    1. Fritz Heinrich Biermann (1872-1958)
    2. Ada Alicia (Biermann) Blume (1874-1959)
    3. Carl Ferdinand Biermann (1876-1950)
    4. Ferdinand Fritz Biermann (1879-1960)
    5. Dorothea Margarethe Caroline (Biermann) Heitman (1881-1971)
    The couple celebrated 68 years of marriage when Friedrich passed away on April 13, 1938, in Steeleville, IL, at the age of 91. Caroline followed him in death five years later on November 14, 1943, in Steeleville, at the age of 89. They were buried in Peace Lutheran Cemetery in Steeleville.

  4. Sophia M. (Biermann) Hartmann (1849-1925)
    5. Sophia was the fourth child and third daughter of Heinrich and Sophie, born January 26, 1849, in Welge. She married Heinrich Friedrich Hartmann (1846-1919) on April 5, 1868, in Bremen. Together they had seven children:
    1. Henry Friedrich Hartmann (1869-1941)
    2. Henry Fritz Hartmann (1872-1875)
    3. Dorothea Sophia Theresa (Hartmann) Schnoeker (1874-1961)
    4. Dorothea Sophia Sarah (Hartmann) Bueckman (1877-1939)
    5. Johann Friedrich George Hartmann (1879-1947)
    6. Sophia Dora Caroline (Hartman) Gremmels (1882-1967)
    7. Heinrich Carl Louis Hartmann (1887-1970)
    The couple celebrated 51 years of marriage when Heinrich passed away on September 28, 1919, in Randolph County, at the age of 72. Sophia followed him in death six years later on October 3, 1925, in Bremen. They were buried in Saint John's Evangelical Lutheran Cemetery in Bremen.

  5. Dorothea Sophia "Doris" (Biermann) Wilson (1851-1930)
    6. Dorothea was the fifth child and fourth daughter of Heinrich and Sophie, born February 13, 1851, in Welge. She married Johann Heinrich Wilson (1848-1929) on July 30, 1871, in Bremen. Together they had four children:
    1. Infant Son (c. 1872)
    2. Johann Friedrich Rudolph Wilson (1873-1955)
    3. Heinrich George Wilson (1875-1953)
    4. Hermann Friedrich Heinrich Wilson (1890-1970)
    The couple celebrated 58 years of marriage when Johann passed away on September 22, 1929, in Bremen, at the age of 80. Dorothea followed him in death only four months later on January 19, 1930, in Randolph County, at the age of 78. They were buried in Saint John's Evangelical Lutheran Cemetery in Bremen.

  6. Friedrich "Fritz" Biermann (1853-1943)
    7. Friedrich was the sixth child and second son of Heinrich and Sophie, born February 10, 1853, in Welge. He married Anna Dorothea Margarethe Meier (1854-1933) on May 7, 1875, in Saint John's Lutheran Church in Bremen. Together they had six children:
    1. Rudolph Fritz Biermann (1876-1955)
    2. Edward F. Biermann (1878-1949)
    3. Heinrich George Biermann (1880-1891)
    4. Emelie "Mela" (Biermann) Ebers (1882-1978)
    5. Louis F. Biermann (1882-1938)
    6. William J. Biermann (1891-1976)
    The couple celebrated 58 years of marriage when Anna passed away on September 24, 1933, in Randolph County, at the age of 78. Friedrich followed her in death ten years later on October 21, 1943, in Randolph County, at the age of 90. They were buried in Saint John's Evangelical Lutheran Cemetery in Bremen.

  7. Carl Friedrich Ludwig Biermann (1856-1939)
    8. Carl was the seventh and last child and third son of Heinrich and Sophie, born February 18, 1856, in Welge. He married Dorothea Sophia Wilson (1857-1935) on September 17, 1880, in Bremen. Together they had nine children:
    1. Ferdinand Frederick Biermann (1881-1957)
    2. John Henry Rudolph Biermann (1883-1977)
    3. Louis Frederick Aaron Biermann (1885-1955)
    4. Catharina Wilhelmina Ida (Biermann) Ernsting (1887-1920)
    5. Frieda Dorothea Sophia (Biermann) Schoeneberg (1889-1949)
    6. Herman George William Biermann (1891-1967)
    7. Dorathea Sophie Lina Marie "Dora" (Biermann) Rabe (1893-1968)
    8. Henry John Carl William Biermann (1895-1974)
    9. Edwin Christopher Heinrich Herman Biermann (1897-1900)
    The couple celebrated 54 years of marriage when Dorothea passed away on February 28, 1935, in Randolph County, at the age of 77. Carl followed her in death four years later on August 17, 1939, in Wine Hill, at the age of 83. They were buried in Saint Paul Lutheran Cemetery in Wine Hill.
